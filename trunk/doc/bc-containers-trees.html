$RCSfile: bc-containers-trees.ads,v $ $Revision: 1.9.2.1 $ $Date: 2002/12/29 16:42:01 $ $Author: simon $ A binary tree is a rooted collection of nodes and arcs, where each node has two children and where arcs may not have cycles or cross-references (as in graphs). A multiway tree is a rooted collection of nodes and arcs, where each node may have an arbitrary number of children and where arcs may not have cycles or cross-references. AVL trees are a form of balance tree (following the algorithm of Adelson-Velskii and Landis) whose behavior only exposes operations to insert, delete, and search for items.
